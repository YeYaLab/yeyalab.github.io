It's not what you look at that matters, it's what you see

— Henry David Thoreau

It is clear that the filters through which we see the world — our beliefs, our emotions, our knowledge — shape our perception, behavior and interaction with other people.

Our lab is interested in studying mechanisms underlying shared and divergent understanding of real-life situations during real-life interactions. Specifically, we are interested in interpersonal synchrony as a mechanism supporting shared understanding and affective response.
